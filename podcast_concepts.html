Frontier

Every episode opens with a single quantitative puzzle anyone can follow in their head. The founder living inside the answer explains how it got there and where it leads.

Format: 2x/month, 90-150 minutes. Occasional 4-episode limited arcs released weekly. Strict rule for first 20 episodes: puzzles are always quantitative.

Season Guide & Guest Tiers

Each season focuses on one frontier. Guests are tiered: Tier A = dream guest, the episode is built around them. Tier B = strong alternate, episode works with slight reframing. Tier C = available backup, concept still holds.

The "Breaks if..." note on each season identifies whether the concept survives without the Tier A guest.

Production Notes

  • Set: Nerdy intellectual interior (books, whiteboards, puzzle printout on table). Cinematic exterior (DP-quality lighting, real lenses, color-graded).
  • Cold open: The puzzle. Always. 30-60 seconds of napkin math the viewer does in their head.
  • Kill criterion for puzzles: "The audience has not seen this before, and once they see it the world looks different."
  • What would kill it: Weak puzzles (math feels rigged) or founder availability (guests who resist the quantitative structure).
