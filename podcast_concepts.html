Frontier

Every episode opens with a single quantitative puzzle anyone can follow in their head. The founder living inside the answer explains how it got there and where it leads.

Format: 2x/month, 90-150 min. Occasional 4-6 episode arcs. First 20 episodes: puzzles always quantitative.

Approach: Map the infrastructure stack of each frontier. Identify who controls each chokepoint. Those people are the guests, because they have the most unique vantage point on the puzzle.

Season 1: Frontier of Compute & Energy
"What does AI cost in 2030, and where does the power physically come from?"

Compute and energy are the same constraint. You can't scale AI without solving both. This season maps the full stack from silicon to socket.

Verdict: Extremely resilient. Does NOT break without any single guest. This season has 26 guests across 8 infrastructure layers. The puzzle ("what does AI cost in 2030, and where does the power come from?") can be answered from any layer. A 6-episode arc could pick one guest per layer and have the deepest season in podcast history. Even without Jensen, C.C. Wei, or Liang (the hardest gets), the grid operators (Pablo Vegas), utility CEOs (Ketchum, Womack), nuclear founders (Hopkins, DeWitte), and the energy policy makers (Wright, Shah) are more accessible and have equally unique vantage points that no other podcast is tapping.

Strongest 6-episode version without any Tier A: Dylan Patel (chip economics) → Chris Miller (geopolitics) → Dario Amodei (demand) → Brian Janous (grid reality) → Jacob DeWitte (nuclear) → Jigar Shah (policy). Every one of these is gettable, and each controls a different chokepoint.

Remaining Seasons (Same Stack-First Approach)

Each season below will be built using the same methodology: map the full infrastructure stack, identify who controls each chokepoint, then tier guests by accessibility.

Season 2: Frontier of Defense (Stack Preview)
"What does it cost to defend a country against autonomous warfare in 2035?"

Infrastructure layers to map:

  • Threat layer: Who defines the threat? (Intelligence community, think tanks, war college historians)
  • Platform builders: Who builds the autonomous systems? (Anduril, Shield AI, Saronic, Skydio, Neros)
  • Munitions supply: Who makes the missiles and warheads? (Raytheon/RTX, Lockheed, L3Harris -- but also new entrants like Castelion)
  • Command & control: Who writes the software that coordinates the platforms? (Palantir, Anduril Lattice, Primer AI)
  • Procurement chokepoint: Who approves what gets bought? (DoD acquisition leadership, OUSD R&E, DIU)
  • Industrial base: Who physically manufactures at scale? (Arsenal 1, Neros factory, legacy primes' production lines)
  • Doctrine: Who decides how autonomous weapons are used in combat? (Joint Chiefs, combatant commanders, NATO)
  • Policy: Who writes the export controls and allied sharing rules? (State Dept, NSC, congressional committees)

Unique guests NOT on the podcast circuit: Heidi Shyu (OUSD R&E), Admiral Christopher Grady (fmr Vice Chairman JCS), Mara Karlin (fmr ASD for Strategy), DIU directors, prime defense CEO CTOs (not the CEO but the person who runs the factory floor).

Season 3: Frontier of Biology (Stack Preview)
"How much does it cost to edit a human genome today vs the year the FDA wrote its rules?"

Infrastructure layers to map:

  • Tools layer: Who makes the gene editing tools? (CRISPR Therapeutics, Beam, Mammoth, Intellia)
  • Synthesis layer: Who synthesizes the DNA? (Twist Bioscience, IDT, GenScript -- Twist controls pricing)
  • Sequencing layer: Who reads the genome? (Illumina CEO, PacBio, Oxford Nanopore -- Illumina is 80% market share)
  • Delivery layer: Who delivers the edit to the right cell? (Viral vector companies, LNP manufacturers -- this is the actual bottleneck)
  • Manufacturing: Who makes cell and gene therapies at scale? (Contract manufacturers, autologous vs allogeneic debate)
  • Regulation: Who approves new therapies? (FDA CBER director Peter Marks, EMA equivalents)
  • Payment: Who pays $2M per treatment? (Insurance, CMS, outcomes-based contracts)
  • Ethics: Who decides where the line is? (Bioethics boards, He Jiankui's legacy, germline editing debate)

Unique guests: Peter Marks (FDA CBER director who approves every gene therapy), Francis deSouza (fmr Illumina CEO who controlled sequencing pricing), the head of a viral vector CDMO (the actual bottleneck nobody talks about).

Season 4: Frontier of Robotics (Stack Preview)
"How many hours of human labor does a humanoid robot need to learn a new task?"

Infrastructure layers to map:

  • Foundation models: Who builds the robot brain? (Physical Intelligence, Covariant, Google DeepMind robotics)
  • Actuators & hardware: Who makes the muscles? (Chinese actuator suppliers, Figure's vertical integration)
  • Simulation: Who builds the training environment? (NVIDIA Omniverse / Isaac, sim-to-real transfer researchers)
  • Platform builders: Who assembles the robot? (Figure, 1X, Apptronik, Agility, Sanctuary, Tesla Optimus)
  • Deployment: Who puts robots in actual workplaces? (Amazon warehouse ops, Zipline logistics, BMW factory ops)
  • Regulation: Who writes robot safety standards? (ISO committees, OSHA, EU AI Act implications)
  • Labor economics: What happens to the workers? (Labor economists, union leaders, retraining programs)

Unique guests: The head of Amazon's robotics division (the largest deployer), the person who runs BMW's robot integration on the assembly line, the chair of the ISO robotics safety committee, a labor economist who models displacement.

Season 5: Frontier of Privacy (Stack Preview)
"What does it cost to surveil one more conversation, at the margin?"

Infrastructure layers to map:

  • Surveillance infrastructure: Who builds the tools? (NSO Group competitors, commercial spyware, lawful intercept vendors)
  • Cryptographic defense: Who builds the shield? (Signal, WhatsApp E2E, post-quantum crypto researchers)
  • Identity layer: Who controls digital identity? (Apple/Google passkeys, government ID systems, Worldcoin)
  • Data brokers: Who trades in personal data? (Acxiom, Clearview AI, social media ad platforms)
  • ZK infrastructure: Who builds privacy-preserving computation? (Ethereum ZK rollups, Aleo, Aztec)
  • Legal framework: Who writes the surveillance rules? (FISA court judges, EU DPA commissioners, GDPR enforcers)
  • Telco layer: Who owns the pipes? (AT&T, Verizon, submarine cable operators -- they see everything)

Unique guests: A FISA court judge (retired), the person who runs AT&T's lawful intercept compliance, a data broker CEO (Acxiom/Oracle Data Cloud), the head of Apple's privacy engineering team, an EU Data Protection Authority commissioner.
