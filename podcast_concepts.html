Frontier

Every episode opens with a single quantitative puzzle anyone can follow in their head. The founder living inside the answer explains how it got there and where it leads.

Format: 2x/month continuous, 90-150 min. Occasional 4-episode limited arcs released weekly. First 20 episodes: puzzles are always quantitative.

Guests are tiered: Tier A = dream guest, episode built around them. Tier B = strong alternate, works with slight reframing. Tier C = available backup, concept holds.

Season 1: Frontier of Compute & Energy
"What does AI cost in 2030, and where does the power physically come from?"
6-episode arc: AI cost curve → chip supply → chip geopolitics → energy demand → energy sources → resolution. Compute and energy are two sides of the same constraint: you can't scale AI without solving both simultaneously.

Production Notes

  • Set: Nerdy intellectual interior (books, whiteboards, puzzle printout on table). Cinematic exterior (DP-quality lighting, real lenses, color-graded).
  • Cold open: The puzzle. Always. 30-60 seconds of napkin math the viewer does in their head.
  • Kill criterion: "The audience has not seen this before, and once they see it the world looks different."
  • What would kill it: Weak puzzles (math feels rigged) or founder availability (guests who resist quantitative structure).
